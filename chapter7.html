Chapter 7: Alternating Current

1. Alternating Current

Detailed Definition: An electric current that reverses its direction many times a second at regular intervals, typically having a sinusoidal waveform.
Hindi Explain: Aisa current jo har thode samay baad apni disha (direction) badalta rehta hai aur jiski value badalti rehti hai.
  • It reverses direction periodically.
  • Easily transmitted over long distances.
  • Voltage can be changed using Transformers.
  • Magnitude changes at every instant.
  • Frequency in India is 50 Hz.
$$ I = I_0 \sin(\omega t) $$

2. Direct Current

Detailed Definition: An electric current flowing in one direction only, which maintains a constant polarity and magnitude over time.
Hindi Explain: Wo current jo hamesha ek hi direction mein behta hai aur change nahi hota, jaise battery ka current.
  • Flows in a single direction.
  • Magnitude remains constant usually.
  • Frequency is zero.
  • Difficult to transmit over very long distances.
  • Obtained from electrochemical cells.
$$ I = \text{Constant} $$

3. Difference between AC and DC

Detailed Definition: AC varies in magnitude and direction periodically, whereas DC flows steadily in one direction without any variation.
Hindi Explain: AC apni disha badalta hai par DC nahi badalta; AC door bhejna sasta hai par DC mehnga padta hai.
  • AC direction changes, DC direction is fixed.
  • AC frequency > 0, DC frequency = 0.
  • AC is dangerous at high voltage, DC is also lethal.
  • AC can be stepped up/down, DC cannot.
  • AC used in homes, DC used in electronics.

4. Time Period

Detailed Definition: The time taken by an alternating current or voltage to complete one full cycle of its variations.
Hindi Explain: AC ko ek poora chakkar (one full cycle) khatam karne mein jitna samay lagta hai.
  • Denoted by letter 'T'.
  • Unit is seconds (s).
  • It is the reciprocal of frequency.
  • Depends on the generator's rotation speed.
  • For 50Hz, T is 0.02 seconds.
$$ T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} $$

5. Frequency

Detailed Definition: The number of complete cycles of alternating current or voltage produced per unit time (usually per second).
Hindi Explain: Ek second mein AC kitne chakkar (cycles) poore kar leti hai, use frequency kehte hain.
  • Unit is Hertz (Hz).
  • India's standard frequency is 50 Hz.
  • Determines the speed of AC machines.
  • High frequency means more cycles per second.
  • Frequency of DC is always zero.
$$ f = \frac{1}{T} $$

6. Angular Frequency

Detailed Definition: The angular displacement per unit time, representing the rate of change of the phase of a sinusoidal wave.
Hindi Explain: AC ki wave kis speed se ghum rahi hai (angle ke hisaab se), use ω batata hai.
  • Unit is rad/sec.
  • Represented by ω (Omega).
  • Related to linear frequency.
  • Used in reactance calculations (L & C).
  • Full rotation is 2π radians.
$$ \omega = 2\pi f = \frac{2\pi}{T} $$

7. AC Voltage

Detailed Definition: An alternating potential difference that changes its magnitude and polarity according to a sine or cosine function of time.
Hindi Explain: Wo voltage jo samay ke saath badalta rehta hai aur direction badalta hai.
  • Changes polarity periodically.
  • Represented by instantaneous value 'v'.
  • Produced by AC generators.
  • Follows a sinusoidal curve.
  • Peak voltage is denoted as $V_0$.
$$ v = V_0 \sin(\omega t) $$

8. AC Current

Detailed Definition: The flow of electric charge that changes its direction and magnitude periodically following a wave pattern.
Hindi Explain: Jab charge ka bahaav badalti direction mein ho, toh use AC Current kehte hain.
  • It varies sinusoidally.
  • Phase depends on circuit components.
  • In pure Resistor, it's in phase with V.
  • Represented as 'i' (instantaneous).
  • Thermal effect works even if direction changes.
$$ i = I_0 \sin(\omega t \pm \phi) $$

9. Sinusoidal Waveform

Detailed Definition: A mathematical curve that describes a smooth periodic oscillation, representing the most fundamental type of alternating current.
Hindi Explain: AC ka graph jo ek chikni lehar (sine curve) ki tarah upar-neeche hota hai.
  • Most basic AC wave shape.
  • Smooth and continuous variation.
  • Positive half and negative half cycles.
  • Zero crossing happens twice per cycle.
  • Peak values at 90° and 270°.

10. Peak Value

Detailed Definition: The maximum positive or negative value reached by an alternating current or voltage during one complete cycle.
Hindi Explain: AC apne raste mein sabse oonchi jahan tak jati hai (max value), use Peak value kehte hain.
  • Symbol: $V_0$ or $I_0$.
  • Also called Amplitude.
  • Occurs at the crest and trough.
  • Twice the RMS value (approx 1.414).
  • Important for insulation safety.
$$ I_0 = I_{rms} \times \sqrt{2} $$

11. Average Value

Detailed Definition: The arithmetic mean of all instantaneous values of AC over a specific period, usually half a cycle.
Hindi Explain: Adhi cycle (half cycle) ke liye AC ki saari values ka औसत (average). Poori cycle mein ye zero hota hai.
  • Full cycle average is zero.
  • Calculated for 0 to T/2 time.
  • About 63.7% of peak value.
  • Used in electrolytic processes.
  • Relation: $0.637 \times \text{Peak}$.
$$ I_{avg} = \frac{2I_0}{\pi} = 0.637 I_0 $$

12. RMS Value

Detailed Definition: The Root Mean Square value is the effective value of AC that produces the same heating effect as a DC of same value in a resistor.
Hindi Explain: AC ki wo effective value jo DC ke barabar heat de. Ghar ke saare meters RMS value hi batate hain.
  • Also called 'Virtual' or 'Effective' value.
  • Most AC meters read this value.
  • Standard home 220V is RMS.
  • 70.7% of peak value.
  • Used for power calculation.
$$ I_{rms} = \frac{I_0}{\sqrt{2}} = 0.707 I_0 $$

1. AC Circuit Elements

Detailed Definition: The basic components used in an AC circuit, namely Resistors (R), Inductors (L), and Capacitors (C), which react differently to alternating current.
Hindi Explain: AC circuit ke wo buniyadi hisse (R, L, C) jo current ke bahaav mein alag-alag tarah se rukawat paida karte hain.
  • Resistor (R) consumes energy as heat.
  • Inductor (L) stores energy in magnetic field.
  • Capacitor (C) stores energy in electric field.
  • Each element has a different effect on phase.
  • Combined effect is called Impedance (Z).

2. Resistor in AC Circuit

Detailed Definition: A circuit containing only a pure resistance connected to an AC source, where Ohm's law applies at every instant.
Hindi Explain: Jab AC source ke saath sirf ek resistor juda ho, toh current aur voltage ek saath chalte hain.
  • Opposes the flow of current.
  • Resistance (R) is independent of frequency.
  • Energy is lost as heat ($I^2R$).
  • Voltage and Current start and end together.
  • Ohm's Law: $V = IR$.
$$ I = \frac{V_m}{R} \sin(\omega t) $$

3. Inductor in AC Circuit

Detailed Definition: A circuit with a pure inductor where the changing AC current induces a back EMF that opposes the current flow.
Hindi Explain: Jab coil (inductor) ko AC se jodte hain, toh wo magnetic field bana kar current ko rokne ki koshish karta hai.
  • Creates an opposition called Inductive Reactance.
  • Does not dissipate power in an ideal case.
  • Current lags behind the voltage.
  • Opposition increases with higher frequency.
  • Used in filters and chokes.
$$ V = L \frac{di}{dt} $$

4. Capacitor in AC Circuit

Detailed Definition: A circuit where an AC source is connected to a capacitor, causing it to constantly charge and discharge.
Hindi Explain: Capacitor AC ko rasta deta hai par DC ko block kar deta hai; ye charge hone ki wajah se current ko lead karwata hai.
  • Current leads the voltage.
  • Opposition decreases as frequency increases.
  • Blocks Direct Current (DC).
  • Stores energy in the electric field.
  • Used for smooth power supply.
$$ X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C} $$

5. Voltage and Current in Phase

Detailed Definition: A condition in a purely resistive circuit where both voltage and current reach their maximum and zero values at the same time.
Hindi Explain: Iska matlab hai ki Voltage aur Current ke beech koi "gap" nahi hai, dono saath-saath upar-neeche hote hain.
  • Phase difference ($\phi$) is zero.
  • Power factor is maximum (1).
  • Seen only in purely resistive circuits.
  • Graphically, both waves overlap in time.
  • Maximum efficiency of power transfer.
$$ \phi = 0^\circ $$

6. Power in Pure Resistive Circuit

Detailed Definition: The rate at which energy is dissipated as heat in a resistor, calculated as the product of RMS voltage and RMS current.
Hindi Explain: Resistor mein jo bijli kharch hoti hai wo garmi ke roop mein nikal jati hai, isse average power nikalte hain.
  • Average power is $V_{rms} \times I_{rms}$.
  • Instanteous power is always positive.
  • All electrical energy is converted to heat.
  • No energy is returned to the source.
  • Power Factor = $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$.
$$ P_{avg} = V_{rms} I_{rms} = I_{rms}^2 R $$

7. Inductive Reactance

Detailed Definition: The opposition offered by an inductor to the flow of alternating current, which is proportional to frequency and inductance.
Hindi Explain: Inductor current ko jo "resistance" dikhata hai use Inductive Reactance kehte hain; frequency badhne par ye badh jati hai.
  • Represented by $X_L$.
  • Unit is Ohm ($\Omega$).
  • Directly proportional to frequency ($f$).
  • $X_L$ is zero for DC ($f=0$).
  • Formula connects $\omega$ and $L$.
$$ X_L = \omega L = 2\pi f L $$

1. AC through Capacitor

Detailed Definition: An AC circuit consisting only of a capacitor where the current is proportional to the rate of change of voltage, causing it to lead the voltage.
Hindi Explain: Jab sirf ek capacitor AC source se juda ho, toh capacitor ke charge hone ki wajah se current, voltage se aage nikal jata hai.
  • Blocks DC but allows AC to pass.
  • Current leads voltage by 90 degrees.
  • Energy is stored in the electric field.
  • No power is dissipated in an ideal capacitor.
  • Opposition offered is called Capacitive Reactance.

2. Phase Difference (Lead)

Detailed Definition: In a purely capacitive circuit, the current reaches its peak value one-fourth of a cycle before the voltage does.
Hindi Explain: Capacitor wale circuit mein current, voltage se $\pi/2$ (90°) pehle apne peak par pahunch jata hai, ise hi "Lead" karna kehte hain.
  • Phase angle $\phi = +90^\circ$ or $+\pi/2$.
  • Current starts from peak when Voltage is zero.
  • Represented by a phasor pointing upwards.
  • Happens because capacitor must charge before voltage builds.
  • Phase factor $\cos(\phi) = 0$.
$$ i = I_0 \sin(\omega t + \pi/2) $$

3. Capacitive Reactance

Detailed Definition: The opposition offered by a capacitor to the flow of AC, which inversely depends on the frequency of the source and the capacitance.
Hindi Explain: Capacitor jo AC ko rokne ke liye rukawat paida karta hai. Frequency jitni zyada hogi, rukawat utni hi kam hogi.
  • Inversely proportional to frequency ($f$).
  • Unit is Ohm ($\Omega$).
  • For DC ($f=0$), $X_c$ is infinite (blocks DC).
  • Decreases as Capacitance ($C$) increases.
  • Helps in filtering low frequencies.
$$ X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C} = \frac{1}{2\pi f C} $$

4. Series LCR Circuit

Detailed Definition: An AC circuit where a resistor (R), an inductor (L), and a capacitor (C) are connected in series with an AC voltage source.
Hindi Explain: Jab ek hi line (series) mein Resistor, Inductor aur Capacitor teeno ko ek saath AC se jod diya jaye.
  • Teeno elements mein same current behta hai.
  • Voltage drop alag-alag hota hai ($V_R, V_L, V_C$).
  • Used in radio and TV tuning circuits.
  • Behavior depends on which reactance is larger.
  • Total opposition is called Impedance ($Z$).

5. Impedance

Detailed Definition: The total effective opposition offered by a combination of L, C, and R elements to the flow of alternating current.
Hindi Explain: LCR circuit ki kul rukawat (Total Resistance) jo current ko milti hai, use Impedance kehte hain.
  • Vector sum of Resistance and Reactance.
  • Unit is Ohm ($\Omega$).
  • Minimum at resonance.
  • Calculated using a right-angled triangle (Impedance Triangle).
  • Formula uses the square of differences between $X_L$ and $X_C$.
$$ Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} $$

6. Phase Angle

Detailed Definition: The angle by which the total voltage leads or lags the total current in an AC circuit, determined by the ratio of reactance to resistance.
Hindi Explain: LCR circuit mein voltage aur current ke beech ka jo final kona (angle) banta hai, use phase angle kehte hain.
  • Represented by $\phi$.
  • If $X_L > X_C$, phase is positive (Inductive).
  • If $X_C > X_L$, phase is negative (Capacitive).
  • Determines the power factor of the circuit.
  • Can be zero at resonance.
$$ \tan \phi = \frac{X_L - X_C}{R} $$

7. Resonance Condition

Detailed Definition: A specific state in a series LCR circuit where the inductive reactance equals the capacitive reactance, resulting in minimum impedance.
Hindi Explain: Wo khaas halat jab $X_L$ aur $X_C$ barabar ho jate hain aur circuit mein current sabse zyada behta hai.
  • $X_L = X_C$ always.
  • Impedance ($Z$) is minimum and equal to $R$.
  • Current reaches its maximum value.
  • Voltage and current come in phase ($\phi = 0$).
  • Circuit behaves as a purely resistive circuit.

8. Resonant Frequency

Detailed Definition: The frequency at which the resonance occurs in an LCR circuit, allowing maximum power to be absorbed from the source.
Hindi Explain: Wo frequency jispar resonance hota hai. Is frequency par signal sabse mazboot pakda jata hai.
  • Depends only on L and C values.
  • Used in tuning radios and mobile signals.
  • Current curve shows a sharp peak here.
  • Independent of resistance $R$.
  • Calculated in Hertz (Hz).
$$ f_r = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} $$

9. Power Factor

Detailed Definition: The ratio of real power to apparent power in an AC circuit, mathematically equal to the cosine of the phase angle.
Hindi Explain: Circuit mein kitni bijli asliyat mein kaam aa rahi hai, ye Power Factor ($\cos \phi$) batata hai.
  • Value ranges from 0 to 1.
  • High power factor means less energy waste.
  • Ratio of Resistance ($R$) to Impedance ($Z$).
  • For pure Resistor, it is 1.
  • For pure L or C, it is 0.
$$ \cos \phi = \frac{R}{Z} $$

10. Wattless Current

Detailed Definition: The component of AC current that flows in a purely inductive or capacitive circuit without consuming any net average power.
Hindi Explain: Wo current jo circuit mein behta toh hai lekin koi bijli (power) kharch nahi karta, use Wattless current kehte hain.
  • Power consumption is zero.
  • Occurs when phase difference is 90 degrees.
  • Energy is just swapped between source and element.
  • Ideal inductors/capacitors draw this current.
  • Mathematically: $I_{rms} \sin \phi$.
$$ P_{avg} = 0 $$

1. Transformer

Detailed Definition: An electrical device that transfers electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction, used to increase or decrease AC voltages.
Hindi Explain: Ek aisi machine jo bina frequency badle voltage ko kam ya zyada kar sakti hai. Ye sirf AC par kaam karta hai.
  • Works only on Alternating Current (AC).
  • Consists of Primary and Secondary coils.
  • Does not change the power (ideally).
  • Frequency remains constant throughout.
  • Used in power grids and electronic adapters.

2. Working Principle

Detailed Definition: It works on the principle of Mutual Induction, where a changing current in the primary coil induces a voltage in the secondary coil.
Hindi Explain: Iska kaam Mutual Induction par tika hai—ek coil mein current badlo toh doosri coil mein bijli paida ho jati hai.
  • Based on Faraday's Law of Induction.
  • Requires a changing magnetic flux.
  • Soft iron core is used to link the flux.
  • No electrical connection between coils.
  • Magnetic field acts as the medium of transfer.
$$ \frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p} = K $$

3. Step-up Transformer

Detailed Definition: A transformer that increases the voltage from primary to secondary by having more turns in the secondary coil than the primary.
Hindi Explain: Ye voltage ko badha deta hai. Isme secondary coil mein zyada pheron (turns) ki sankhya hoti hai.
  • $V_s > V_p$ (Output voltage is higher).
  • $N_s > N_p$ (Secondary has more turns).
  • Current decreases as voltage increases.
  • Used in power stations for transmission.
  • Transformation ratio $K > 1$.

4. Step-down Transformer

Detailed Definition: A transformer that decreases the voltage from primary to secondary by having fewer turns in the secondary coil compared to the primary.
Hindi Explain: Ye high voltage ko kam karta hai. Hamare gharon ke paas lage transformers yahi hote hain.
  • $V_s < V_p$ (Output voltage is lower).
  • $N_s < N_p$ (Primary has more turns).
  • Current increases as voltage decreases.
  • Used in mobile chargers and doorbells.
  • Transformation ratio $K < 1$.

5. Losses in Transformer

Detailed Definition: The reduction in energy efficiency due to heat, magnetic flux leakage, and resistance within the transformer components.
Hindi Explain: Transformer mein energy ka nuksan garmi aur magnetic leakage ki wajah se hota hai.
  • Copper Loss: Heat in coils due to resistance.
  • Iron/Core Loss: Eddy currents and hysteresis.
  • Flux Leakage: Not all flux reaches secondary.
  • Humming Loss: Energy lost as sound/vibration.
  • Laminated cores help reduce these losses.

6. Efficiency (η)

Detailed Definition: The ratio of the output power delivered to the secondary circuit to the input power supplied to the primary circuit.
Hindi Explain: Transformer kitna accha kaam kar raha hai (Output aur Input ka ratio). Ideal transformer ki efficiency 100% hoti hai.
  • Represented by η (Eta).
  • Expressed as a percentage.
  • Real transformers have 90-99% efficiency.
  • $\text{Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Input Power}}$.
  • High efficiency means less heat generation.
$$ \eta = \frac{V_s I_s}{V_p I_p} \times 100\% $$

Q8. Show that in a purely inductive circuit, current lags voltage by 90 degrees.

Derivation:
1. Applied AC voltage: V = V0 sin ωt.
2. Back EMF in inductor: e = -L(dI/dt).
3. By Kirchhoff's law: V + e = 0 ⇒ V = L(dI/dt).
4. dI = (V/L) dt = (V0/L) sin(ωt) dt.
5. Integrating both sides: I = -(V0/ωL) cos(ωt).
6. Use identity: -cosθ = sin(θ - π/2).
7. I = I0 sin(ωt - π/2), where I0 = V0/ωL.
8. Inductive Reactance: XL = ωL.
9. Comparison shows phase difference is -π/2.
10. Conclusion: Current lags voltage by 90°.
