SMART STUDY FOR TOPPERS

1. Nature of Light

English: Light is a form of energy that enables us to see objects.
Hindi: Light energy ka ek roop hai jo humein cheezon ko dekhne mein madad karta hai.
  • It travels in a straight line path.
  • The speed of light in vacuum is $3 \times 10^8$ m/s.
  • Light does not require any material medium for propagation.
  • It exhibits dual nature: wave and particle.
$$ c = 3 \times 10^8 \, m/s $$

2. Ray of Light

English: The direction or path along which light energy travels.
Hindi: Wo disha ya rasta jis par light chalti hai use Ray kehte hain.
  • Represented by a straight line with an arrow.
  • The arrow represents the direction of propagation.
  • A bundle of rays is called a beam of light.
  • Light rays always follow the shortest path.
$$ \text{Ray} \rightarrow \text{Beam} $$

3. Rectilinear Propagation

English: Light travels in a straight line in a homogeneous medium.
Hindi: Ek hi tarah ke medium mein light hamesha seedhi line mein chalti hai.
  • Formation of shadows is a result of this property.
  • Occurence of eclipses depends on this principle.
  • Pinhole camera works based on rectilinear propagation.
  • Light bends only when the medium changes.
$$ \text{Straight Line Path} $$

4. Reflection of Light

English: Bouncing back of light when it strikes a polished surface.
Hindi: Jab light kisi chamkili satah se takra kar wapas lout-ti hai.
  • Mirrors are the most common examples of reflectors.
  • Smooth surfaces show regular reflection.
  • Rough surfaces show diffused or irregular reflection.
  • Silvering is done on glass to make it a mirror.
$$ \text{Bounce Back} $$

5. Laws of Reflection

English: Rules that light rays follow during the process of reflection.
Hindi: Reflection ke waqt light rays jin niyamon ka palan karti hain.
  • The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
  • Incident ray, normal, and reflected ray lie in the same plane.
  • These laws are valid for all reflecting surfaces.
  • Normal is drawn perpendicular to the surface.
$$ \angle i = \angle r $$

6. Plane Mirror

English: A mirror with a flat reflecting surface that forms virtual images.
Hindi: Ek bilkul seedhi satah wala mirror jo virtual image banata hai.
  • Focal length of a plane mirror is infinity.
  • Power of a plane mirror is always zero.
  • Image distance equals object distance.
  • The image formed is always erect.
$$ f = \infty $$

7. Image Formation in Plane Mirror

English: Process of creating an image of an object using a flat mirror.
Hindi: Plane mirror dwara kisi object ki photo banane ki prakriya.
  • The image is virtual (cannot be taken on screen).
  • The size of the image is equal to the size of the object.
  • Magnification produced is exactly +1.
  • Image is formed as far behind as the object is in front.
$$ m = +1 $$

8. Lateral Inversion

English: Reversal of left and right sides in a mirror image.
Hindi: Mirror mein baayein ka daayein aur daayein ka baayein dikhna.
  • Left side of the object appears as the right side of image.
  • This is why AMBULANCE is written in reverse.
  • The image is not inverted vertically (upside down).
  • Only the lateral (side) orientation changes.
$$ \text{Left} \leftrightarrow \text{Right} $$

1. Spherical Mirrors

English: A mirror whose reflecting surface is a part of a hollow sphere.
Hindi: Wo mirror jiski chamkili satah kisi khokhle gole ka ek hissa hoti hai.
  • Two types: Concave and Convex mirrors.
  • Follows laws of reflection like plane mirrors.
  • Used to converge or diverge light rays.
  • One side is silvered/opaque for reflection.
$$ R = 2f $$

2. Concave Mirror

English: A spherical mirror whose reflecting surface is curved inwards.
Hindi: Wo mirror jiski chamkili satah andar ki taraf dhasi hui hoti hai.
  • Also known as a converging mirror.
  • Forms both real and virtual images.
  • Used in car headlights and shaving mirrors.
  • Parallel rays meet at a point after reflection.
$$ \text{Converging Mirror} $$

3. Convex Mirror

English: A spherical mirror whose reflecting surface is curved outwards.
Hindi: Wo mirror jiski chamkili satah bahar ki taraf ubhri hui hoti hai.
  • Also known as a diverging mirror.
  • Always forms virtual, erect, and small images.
  • Used as rear-view mirrors in vehicles.
  • Provides a much wider field of view.
$$ \text{Diverging Mirror} $$

4. Principal Axis

English: The straight line passing through the Pole and Center of Curvature.
Hindi: Pole aur Center of Curvature se guzarne wali seedhi rekha.
  • Perpendicular to the mirror at its pole.
  • Acts as a reference for measuring distances.
  • Parallel incident rays meet on this axis.
  • Extends infinitely on both sides.
$$ \text{CP Line} $$

5. Pole

English: The center point of the reflecting surface of a spherical mirror.
Hindi: Spherical mirror ki chamkili satah ka bilkul center bindu.
  • Represented by the letter 'P'.
  • Lies directly on the surface of the mirror.
  • Used as the origin for all distance measurements.
  • Located at the center of the mirror's aperture.
$$ \text{Point P} $$

6. Centre of Curvature

English: The center of the sphere of which the mirror is a part.
Hindi: Us gole ka kendra jiska mirror ek chhota hissa hai.
  • Represented by the letter 'C'.
  • It is not a part of the mirror's surface.
  • Ray passing through 'C' reflects back on same path.
  • Position depends on the type of mirror.
$$ \text{Point C} $$

7. Radius of Curvature

English: The distance between the Pole and Center of Curvature.
Hindi: Mirror ke Pole aur Center of Curvature ke beech ki doori.
  • Represented by the letter 'R'.
  • Equal to the radius of the original sphere.
  • $R = 2f$ (twice the focal length).
  • Measured along the principal axis.
$$ R = CP $$

8. Principal Focus

English: Point where parallel rays meet or appear to meet after reflection.
Hindi: Wo bindu jahan parallel rays reflection ke baad milti hain.
  • Represented by the letter 'F'.
  • Lies exactly midway between P and C.
  • Real for Concave, Virtual for Convex.
  • Essential for ray diagram construction.
$$ \text{Point F} $$

9. Focal Length

English: The distance between the Pole and the Principal Focus.
Hindi: Mirror ke Pole aur Focus ke beech ki doori.
  • Represented by the letter 'f'.
  • $f = R/2$ (half of radius of curvature).
  • Negative for Concave, Positive for Convex.
  • Determines the convergence/divergence power.
$$ f = \frac{R}{2} $$

10. Ray Diagrams (Concave)

English: Visualization of image formation for concave mirrors.
Hindi: Concave mirror dwara image banane ka chitra.
  • 6 cases based on object distance.
  • Produces real, inverted, and varied size images.
  • Virtual image only when object is between P and F.
  • Ray through 'C' reflects back along same path.
$$ \text{6 Image Cases} $$

11. Ray Diagrams (Convex)

English: Visualization of image formation for convex mirrors.
Hindi: Convex mirror dwara image banane ka chitra.
  • Mainly 2 cases: Infinity and finite distance.
  • Always forms virtual, erect, and small images.
  • Image always forms behind the mirror.
  • Parallel rays appear to diverge from 'F'.
$$ \text{Always Virtual} $$

12. Mirror Formula

English: Relation between object distance, image distance and focal length.
Hindi: u, v aur f ke beech ka mathematical sambandh.
  • $u$ = Object distance (always negative).
  • $v$ = Image distance.
  • $f$ = Focal length of the mirror.
  • Works for both Concave and Convex mirrors.
$$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} $$

13. Magnification

English: Ratio of height of image to height of object.
Hindi: Image aur object ki unchai ka anupaat (ratio).
  • If $m$ is negative, image is real and inverted.
  • If $m$ is positive, image is virtual and erect.
  • $m = 1$ means image is same size as object.
  • Also related to distances: $m = -v/u$.
$$ m = \frac{h'}{h} = -\frac{v}{u} $$

1. Refraction of Light

English: The bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.
Hindi: Jab light ek medium se dusre medium mein jati hai, toh uska mudna.
  • Occurs due to change in speed of light.
  • Bends towards normal in denser medium.
  • Bends away from normal in rarer medium.
  • Frequency remains constant during refraction.
$$ \text{Bending of Light} $$

2. Laws of Refraction

English: Rules followed by light while changing its path between media.
Hindi: Wo niyam jo light refraction ke waqt follow karti hai.
  • Incident ray, refracted ray, and normal lie in same plane.
  • Second law is also known as Snell's Law.
  • Ratio of $\sin i$ to $\sin r$ is always constant.
  • This constant is the refractive index.
$$ \frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = \text{constant} $$

3. Refractive Index

English: Measure of how much light slows down in a medium.
Hindi: Ye batata hai ki light kisi medium mein kitni dheemi hoti hai.
  • Represented by the symbol '$n$' or '$\mu$'.
  • It is a unitless and dimensionless quantity.
  • Higher index means light bends more.
  • Depends on the optical density of material.
$$ n = \frac{c}{v} $$

4. Absolute Refractive Index

English: Refractive index of a medium with respect to vacuum.
Hindi: Vacuum ke muqable kisi medium ka refractive index.
  • Speed of light in vacuum ($c$) is $3 \times 10^8$ m/s.
  • Value is always greater than or equal to 1.
  • For Diamond, it is approx 2.42.
  • Indicates overall optical density.
$$ n_m = \frac{c}{v_m} $$

5. Relative Refractive Index

English: Refractive index of medium 2 with respect to medium 1.
Hindi: Pehle medium ke muqable dusre medium ka index.
  • Compares speed of light in two different media.
  • Formula is $n_{21} = v_1 / v_2$.
  • Can be less than or greater than 1.
  • Used for glass-to-water calculations.
$$ n_{21} = \frac{n_2}{n_1} $$

6. Snell’s Law

English: The ratio $\sin i / \sin r$ is constant for a pair of media.
Hindi: Do mediums ke liye $\sin i$ aur $\sin r$ ka ratio fix hota hai.
  • Fundamental law for all refraction cases.
  • Constant value equals the relative refractive index.
  • If $i = 0^\circ$, then $r = 0^\circ$ (no bending).
  • Valid for light of a specific color.
$$ n_1 \sin i = n_2 \sin r $$

7. Optical Density

English: The ability of a medium to refract or bend light.
Hindi: Kisi medium ki light ko modne ki kshamta.
  • Different from mass (physical) density.
  • Denser medium = Slower speed of light.
  • Rarer medium = Higher speed of light.
  • Kerosene is optically denser than water.
$$ \text{Speed} \propto \frac{1}{\text{Density}} $$

8. Lateral Displacement

English: Perpendicular distance between incident and emergent rays.
Hindi: Incident aur emergent rays ke beech ki seedhi doori.
  • Observed in rectangular glass slabs.
  • Increases with thickness of the slab.
  • Increases with the angle of incidence.
  • Depends on the refractive index.
$$ \text{Lateral Shift} $$

9. Emergent Ray

English: The ray of light that finally comes out of a medium.
Hindi: Wo kiran jo medium se bahar nikal jati hai.
  • Parallel to incident ray in a glass slab.
  • Angle of emergence ($e$) equals angle of incidence ($i$).
  • Undergoes lateral shift from original path.
  • Moves into the surrounding medium (usually air).
$$ \angle i = \angle e $$

10. Total Internal Reflection

English: Complete reflection of a ray of light within a medium back into the same medium.
Hindi: Jab light ki kiran poori tarah se takra kar wapas usi medium mein laut jati hai.
  • Light must travel from a denser to a rarer medium.
  • Angle of incidence must be greater than the critical angle.
  • No light is refracted; 100% light is reflected back.
  • Responsible for the sparkling of diamonds.
$$ \angle i > \angle C $$

11. Critical Angle

English: The angle of incidence for which the angle of refraction is 90°.
Hindi: Wo incidence angle jis par refraction angle $90^\circ$ ho jata hai.
  • At this angle, the refracted ray grazes the boundary surface.
  • Depends on the refractive index of the two media.
  • Symbolized as $C$ or $\theta_c$.
  • If $i$ increases beyond this, TIR occurs.
$$ \sin C = \frac{1}{n} $$

12. Conditions for TIR

English: Necessary requirements for total internal reflection to happen.
Hindi: TIR hone ke liye zaroori shartein.
  • Light must move from Optically Denser to Rarer medium.
  • Angle of incidence ($i$) > Critical angle ($C$).
  • The interface must be transparent.
  • If either condition fails, refraction or simple reflection occurs.
$$ \text{Denser } \rightarrow \text{ Rarer} $$

13. Optical Fibre

English: A thin glass/plastic fiber used to transmit light signals using TIR.
Hindi: Ek patli glass ki taar jo light signals ko TIR ke zariye bhejti hai.
  • Consists of a Core (high $n$) and Cladding (low $n$).
  • Signals travel at the speed of light with very little loss.
  • Used extensively in high-speed internet and telecommunications.
  • Also used in endoscopy (medical imaging).
$$ \text{Based on TIR Principle} $$

14. Working of Optical Fibre

English: Light undergoes multiple TIRs inside the core to reach the end.
Hindi: Light core ke andar baar-baar TIR karke aage badhti hai.
  • Light enters at one end at a specific angle.
  • It strikes the core-cladding boundary at $i > C$.
  • It keeps bouncing inside until it exits from the other end.
  • Even if the fiber is bent, the signal follows the curve.
$$ \text{Multiple TIRs} $$

15. Refraction at Curved Surface

English: Refraction of light through spherical boundaries like glass spheres.
Hindi: Golaakaar (curved) satah se light ka refraction.
  • Applies to single spherical surfaces separating two media.
  • The normal is always the radius passing through the center.
  • Follows specific sign conventions for $u$, $v$, and $R$.
  • Foundation for understanding how lenses work.
$$ \frac{n_2}{v} - \frac{n_1}{u} = \frac{n_2 - n_1}{R} $$

16. Lens

English: A transparent material bound by two surfaces, at least one being curved.
Hindi: Ek transparent cheez jiski kam se kam ek satah curved ho.
  • Convex Lens: Thick at center, thin at edges (Converging).
  • Concave Lens: Thin at center, thick at edges (Diverging).
  • Used in spectacles, cameras, and microscopes.
  • Forms images by the process of refraction.
$$ \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} $$

17. Convex Lens

English: A lens that is thicker at the center than at the edges; it converges light rays.
Hindi: Wo lens jo beech mein se mota aur kinaro se patla hota hai.
  • Also known as a Converging Lens.
  • Can form both real and virtual images.
  • Used in magnifying glasses and cameras.
  • It has a positive focal length ($+f$).
$$ \text{Converging Action} $$

18. Concave Lens

English: A lens that is thinner at the center than at the edges; it diverges light rays.
Hindi: Wo lens jo beech mein se patla aur kinaro se mota hota hai.
  • Also known as a Diverging Lens.
  • Always forms a virtual, erect, and diminished image.
  • Used in spectacles for correcting Myopia.
  • It has a negative focal length ($-f$).
$$ \text{Diverging Action} $$

19. Optical Centre

English: The central point of a lens through which light passes without deviation.
Hindi: Lens ka wo beech ka point jahan se light bina mude nikal jati hai.
  • Represented by the symbol '$O$'.
  • Any ray passing through it does not bend.
  • All distances in lenses are measured from here.
  • Located on the principal axis.
$$ \text{Point O} $$

20. Principal Focus

English: Point where parallel rays meet or appear to meet after refraction.
Hindi: Wo point jahan parallel rays refraction ke baad milti hain.
  • A lens has two principal foci ($F_1$ and $F_2$).
  • Real for Convex lens, Virtual for Concave lens.
  • It lies on the principal axis.
  • Light appears to diverge from it in concave lenses.
$$ F_1, F_2 $$

21. Focal Length

English: The distance between the optical centre and the principal focus.
Hindi: Optical centre aur focus ke beech ki doori.
  • Represented by '$f$'.
  • Positive for Convex, Negative for Concave.
  • Determines how strongly the lens bends light.
  • SI unit is meters (m).
$$ f = \text{Distance}(O-F) $$

22. Ray Diagrams (Convex)

English: Visual representation of images formed by a convex lens.
Hindi: Convex lens dwara image banne ka diagram.
  • Image can be real, inverted, or virtual.
  • Virtual image forms when object is between F and O.
  • Ray through $O$ goes straight.
  • Parallel ray passes through Focus.
$$ \text{6 Image Cases} $$

23. Ray Diagrams (Concave)

English: Visual representation of images formed by a concave lens.
Hindi: Concave lens dwara image banne ka diagram.
  • Always forms virtual and erect images.
  • Image is always smaller than the object.
  • Image is always between F and O.
  • Light rays appear to diverge from Focus.
$$ \text{Always Virtual} $$

24. Lens Formula

English: Relation between object distance, image distance, and focal length.
Hindi: u, v aur f ke beech ka sambandh.
  • $u$ is object distance, $v$ is image distance.
  • Uses Cartesian sign convention.
  • Different from mirror formula (minus sign).
  • Valid for all types of spherical lenses.
$$ \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$

25. Magnification

English: Ratio of height of image to height of object.
Hindi: Image ki unchai aur object ki unchai ka anupaat.
  • Positive 'm' means virtual and erect image.
  • Negative 'm' means real and inverted image.
  • Also expressed as the ratio $v/u$.
  • No units as it is a ratio.
$$ m = \frac{h'}{h} = \frac{v}{u} $$

26. Power of Lens

English: Degree of convergence or divergence of light rays.
Hindi: Lens ki light ko modne ki kshamta.
  • It is the reciprocal of focal length.
  • $P = 1/f$ when $f$ is in meters.
  • More power means more bending.
  • Power is positive for Convex lens.
$$ P = \frac{1}{f(m)} $$

27. Unit of Power (Dioptre)

English: The SI unit of power of a lens.
Hindi: Lens ki power napne ki SI unit.
  • Represented by the letter 'D'.
  • 1D is the power of a lens with $f = 1$ meter.
  • Calculated using $f$ in meters only.
  • Concave lens has negative power in Dioptres.
$$ 1D = 1m^{-1} $$

28. Combination of Lenses

English: Placing two or more lenses in contact to increase magnification or sharpen the image.
Hindi: Do ya zyada lenses ko ek saath rakhna taaki power ya clarity badh sake.
  • The net power ($P$) is the algebraic sum of individual powers.
  • Used in cameras, microscopes, and telescopes.
  • If one lens is convex ($+P$) and another concave ($-P$), they can cancel out.
  • Total magnification ($m$) is the product of individual magnifications ($m_1 \times m_2$).
$$ P = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + \dots $$

29. Optical Instruments

English: Devices that use mirrors and lenses to assist the eye in seeing objects.
Hindi: Wo tools jo mirrors aur lenses ka istemal karke dekhne mein madad karte hain.
  • Simple Microscope: Uses a single convex lens of short focal length.
  • Compound Microscope: Uses two convex lenses (Objective and Eyepiece).
  • Telescope: Used to see distant objects like stars or planets.
  • Projectors and Cameras are also common optical instruments.
$$ \text{Microscope / Telescope} $$

30. Structure of Eye

English: The biological organ responsible for vision, acting like a natural camera.
Hindi: Insaani aankh ki banawat jo ek natural camera ki tarah kaam karti hai.
  • Cornea: Front transparent part that refracts most of the light.
  • Iris & Pupil: Control the amount of light entering the eye.
  • Eye Lens: A crystalline convex lens that focuses light on the retina.
  • Retina: The screen where the image is formed (contains light-sensitive cells).
$$ \text{Natural Convex Lens} $$

31. Power of Accommodation

English: The ability of the eye lens to adjust its focal length to see near and far objects clearly.
Hindi: Aankh ke lens ki focal length badalne ki kshamta taaki door aur paas ka saaf dikhe.
  • Ciliary muscles help in changing the curvature of the eye lens.
  • To see far: Muscles relax, lens becomes thin (focal length increases).
  • To see near: Muscles contract, lens becomes thick (focal length decreases).
  • This ability decreases with age (Presbyopia).
$$ \text{Focal Length Adjustment} $$

32. Near Point

English: The minimum distance at which the eye can see objects clearly without strain.
Hindi: Wo kam se kam doori jahan aankh bina kisi dabaav ke saaf dekh sakti hai.
  • Also called the "Least Distance of Distinct Vision".
  • For a normal adult eye, this distance is 25 cm.
  • Closer than this, the image appears blurred or causes eye strain.
  • It can increase with age or due to Hypermetropia.
$$ D = 25 \, \text{cm} $$

33. Far Point

English: The maximum distance up to which the eye can see objects clearly.
Hindi: Wo zyada se zyada doori jahan tak aankh saaf dekh sakti hai.
  • For a normal human eye, the far point is at Infinity ($\infty$).
  • A healthy eye can see stars (which are very far) clearly.
  • In Myopia (nearsightedness), the far point shifts closer to the eye.
  • Corrected using a concave lens to shift the image back to the retina.
$$ \text{Far Point} = \infty $$

34. Myopia (Short Sightedness)

English: A defect where a person can see nearby objects clearly but cannot see distant objects.
Hindi: Isme insaan ko paas ka saaf dikhta hai, lekin door ka dhundhla (blur) dikhta hai.
  • Image is formed in front of the retina instead of on it.
  • Causes: Excessive curvature of eye lens or elongation of eyeball.
  • Far point of the eye becomes less than infinity.
  • Corrected using a Concave lens.
$$ \text{Concave Lens Required} $$

35. Hypermetropia

English: A defect where a person can see distant objects clearly but cannot see nearby objects.
Hindi: Isme door ka saaf dikhta hai, par paas ki cheezein dhundhli dikhti hain.
  • Image is formed behind the retina.
  • Causes: Focal length of eye lens is too long or eyeball is too small.
  • Near point of the eye shifts further away from 25 cm.
  • Corrected using a Convex lens.
$$ \text{Convex Lens Required} $$

36. Presbyopia

English: Age-related loss of the eye's ability to focus on nearby objects.
Hindi: Umra badhne ke saath paas ki nazar kamzor hona.
  • Occurs due to weakening of ciliary muscles and loss of lens flexibility.
  • Person may suffer from both myopia and hypermetropia.
  • Often corrected using Bi-focal lenses.
  • Upper part is concave (for distance) and lower is convex (for reading).
$$ \text{Bi-focal Lenses} $$

37. Correction of Myopia

English: Using a concave lens of suitable power to shift the image back to the retina.
Hindi: Concave lens laga kar image ko wapas retina par lana.
  • The lens diverges incoming rays so they focus correctly on retina.
  • Power of correcting lens is negative ($-D$).
  • Focal length of lens matches the distance to the eye's new far point.
  • Restores clear vision for distant objects.
$$ P = -\frac{1}{f} $$

38. Correction of Hypermetropia

English: Using a convex lens to increase converging power of the eye.
Hindi: Convex lens se light ko pehle modna taaki image retina par ban jaye.
  • Convex lens provides additional converging power.
  • Power of correcting lens is positive ($+D$).
  • Helps the eye to focus on objects at 25 cm again.
  • The virtual image of the object is formed at the eye's actual near point.
$$ P = +\frac{1}{f} $$

39. Simple Microscope

English: A magnifying glass consisting of a single convex lens of short focal length.
Hindi: Ek akela convex lens jo cheezon ko bada karke dikhata hai.
  • Object is placed within the focal length ($u < f$).
  • Forms a virtual, erect, and magnified image.
  • Image is formed at the least distance of distinct vision ($D$).
  • Commonly used by watchmakers and jewelers.
$$ \text{Virtual & Enlarged Image} $$

40. Magnifying Power

English: The ratio of the angle subtended by the image at the eye to the angle subtended by the object.
Hindi: Ye batata hai ki koi instrument cheez ko kitna bada dikha sakta hai.
  • Represented by '$M$'.
  • For Simple Microscope: $M = 1 + D/f$ (when image is at $D$).
  • For Simple Microscope: $M = D/f$ (when image is at infinity).
  • Increasing the curvature of the lens increases magnifying power.
$$ M = 1 + \frac{D}{f} $$

41. Compound Microscope

English: An optical instrument used to see high magnification of tiny objects using two convex lenses.
Hindi: Bahut chhoti cheezon ko bada karke dekhne wala yantra jisme do convex lenses hote hain.
  • Uses two lenses: Objective (small $f$) and Eyepiece (larger $f$).
  • The objective lens forms a real, inverted, and magnified image.
  • The eyepiece acts as a simple magnifying glass for the first image.
  • Final image is virtual, inverted, and highly magnified.
$$ \text{Two-stage Magnification} $$

42. Magnifying Power (Microscope)

English: The total magnification produced by the combination of objective and eyepiece.
Hindi: Objective aur Eyepiece lens milkar cheez ko kitna bada dikhate hain.
  • Total $M = m_o \times m_e$.
  • For image at $D$: $M = \frac{L}{f_o} (1 + \frac{D}{f_e})$.
  • Short focal lengths of both lenses increase the magnification.
  • $L$ is the length of the microscope tube.
$$ M = m_o \times m_e $$

43. Astronomical Telescope

English: An instrument used to see distant celestial objects like stars and planets.
Hindi: Door ki khagoliya cheezein (tare, grah) dekhne wala yantra.
  • Uses two convex lenses: Objective (large aperture) and Eyepiece (small $f$).
  • Objective lens collects maximum light from distant objects.
  • The intermediate image is real and inverted.
  • The final image is virtual and inverted with respect to the object.
$$ \text{For Distant Objects} $$

44. Magnifying Power (Telescope)

English: The ratio of the angle subtended at the eye by the image to the angle subtended by the distant object.
Hindi: Door ki cheez ka aankh par banne wala angle aur image ke angle ka ratio.
  • For normal adjustment (image at infinity): $M = f_o / f_e$.
  • Length of the tube in normal adjustment is $L = f_o + f_e$.
  • To increase $M$, the focal length of the objective ($f_o$) should be large.
  • Final image is at a large distance from the eye for relaxed vision.
$$ M = \frac{f_o}{f_e} $$

Q12. What is Hypermetropia? Discuss its causes and method of correction.

Full 10-Step Analysis:

2. The near point of the eye shifts farther away from 25 cm.

10. This allows the final image to be formed perfectly on the retina.

Q15. Explain the construction and working of an Optical Fibre.

1. An optical fibre is a very thin strand of high-quality glass or quartz.
2. It consists of three main parts: Core, Cladding, and Buffer Coating.
3. The Core (inner part) has a higher refractive index (n1).

9. It can carry light signals over several kilometers even if the fibre is bent.
