1. Bar Magnet

English: A rectangular object that possesses permanent magnetic properties.
Hindi: Ek rectangular tukda jisme hamesha rehne wali magnetic shakti hoti hai.
  • It has two poles: North Pole and South Pole.
  • Always points North-South when suspended freely.
  • Like poles repel, unlike poles attract each other.
  • Magnetic strength is highest at the ends (poles).
$$ M = m \times 2l $$

2. Properties of Bar Magnet

English: The characteristic behaviors exhibited by a bar magnet.
Hindi: Bar magnet ke wo gun jo use dusre padarthon se alag banate hain.
  • Attractive Property: It attracts ferromagnetic materials.
  • Directive Property: It aligns with Earth's magnetic field.
  • Poles exist in pairs: Monopoles cannot exist.
  • Inductive Property: It can induce magnetism in others.
$$ F = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} $$

3. Magnetic Poles

English: The regions at the ends of a magnet where magnetism is strongest.
Hindi: Magnet ke kinaro wala wo hissa jahan magnetic power sabse zyada hoti hai.
  • Every magnet must have at least two poles.
  • North pole is where field lines emerge.
  • South pole is where field lines enter.
  • Poles cannot be separated by cutting the magnet.
$$ Pole \, Strength = m $$

4. Magnetic Axis

English: The imaginary straight line passing through the two poles of a magnet.
Hindi: Magnet ke dono poles ke beech se guzarne wali kalpanik seedhi rekha.
  • It represents the orientation of the magnet.
  • Magnetic length is measured along this axis.
  • Magnetic field along the axis is called axial field.
  • It is longer than the actual physical length of the bar.
$$ B_{axial} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{2M}{r^3} $$

5. Magnetic Dipole

English: A pair of two equal and opposite poles separated by a distance.
Hindi: Do barabar aur vipreet poles ka joda jo thodi doori par ho.
  • A bar magnet is a perfect example of a dipole.
  • It produces a magnetic field in its surrounding space.
  • Magnetic dipole moment is a vector quantity.
  • Atomic loops of current also act as dipoles.
$$ \vec{\tau} = \vec{M} \times \vec{B} $$

6. Magnetic Field Lines

English: Imaginary curves used to represent the magnetic field.
Hindi: Magnetic field ko darshane ke liye banayi gayi kalpanik curve rekhayein.
  • They start from North and end at South pole outside.
  • They form continuous closed loops (N to S to N).
  • Two field lines never intersect each other.
  • Density of lines shows the strength of the field.
$$ \phi = \int \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} $$

7. Properties of Field Lines

English: The specific characteristics of magnetic field lines.
Hindi: Magnetic field lines ki wo khasiyat jo unki disha aur taqat batati hai.
  • Tangent at any point gives the field direction.
  • They are crowded near poles where the field is strong.
  • Inside the magnet, lines travel from South to North.
  • They behave like stretched elastic strings.
$$ \vec{B} = \text{Field Vector} $$

8. Field Lines of Bar Magnet

English: The specific pattern of lines created by a single bar magnet.
Hindi: Ek bar magnet dwara banaye gaye magnetic field ka nishchit pattern.
  • Lines are symmetrical about the magnetic axis.
  • They are most concentrated at the pole tips.
  • They spread out as they move away from the magnet.
  • They represent a non-uniform magnetic field.
$$ B = \frac{Force}{m} $$

1. Magnetic Dipole Moment

Detailed English: Magnetic Dipole Moment is defined as the product of the strength of either magnetic pole (m) and the magnetic length (2l) between them. It measures the strength and orientation of a magnet.
Hinglish Detail: Magnetic Dipole Moment ka matlab hota hai magnet ke kisi ek pole ki shakti (pole strength) aur dono poles ke beech ki magnetic doori (length) ka guna. Yeh magnet ki power ko dikhata hai.
  • It is a vector quantity (magnitude aur direction dono hote hain).
  • Its direction is always from South to North pole internally.
  • Formula: $M = m \times 2l$, jahan 'm' pole strength hai.
$$ M = m \times 2l $$

2. Direction of Magnetic Moment

Detailed English: The direction of the magnetic dipole moment vector points from the South Pole to the North Pole along the magnetic axis of the magnet.
Hinglish Detail: Magnetic moment ki disha hamesha magnet ke andar South pole se North pole ki taraf hoti hai. Yeh hamesha magnetic axis ke parallel chalti hai.
  • For current loops, it is determined by the Right-Hand Thumb Rule.
  • It helps in aligning the magnet in an external magnetic field.
$$ \vec{M}: S \rightarrow N $$

3. Unit of Magnetic Moment

Detailed English: The SI unit of magnetic dipole moment is Ampere-meter square ($A \cdot m^2$). It can also be expressed in terms of energy as Joules per Tesla ($J/T$).
Hinglish Detail: Magnetic moment ko napne ke liye SI unit Ampere-meter square ($A \cdot m^2$) ka use hota hai. Iski ek aur unit Joule per Tesla ($J/T$) bhi hoti hai.
  • SI Unit: $A \cdot m^2$.
  • Dimensions: $[L^2 A]$.
  • It is a derived quantity from current and area.
$$ Unit = A \cdot m^2 \text{ or } J/T $$

4. Earth’s Magnetism

Detailed English: The Earth behaves like a giant bar magnet. This is likely due to the "Dynamo Effect" caused by the motion of molten iron and nickel in its outer core.
Hinglish Detail: Prithvi ek bahut bade bar magnet ki tarah kaam karti hai. Iska magnetic field core mein moujood pighle hue lohe (molten iron) ki movement ki wajah se banta hai.
  • Earth's magnetic poles are near but not exactly at the geographic poles.
  • A magnetic compass needle aligns itself with Earth's magnetic lines.
$$ B_{earth} \approx 0.3 \times 10^{-4} T $$

5. Magnetic Elements

Detailed English: Magnetic elements are parameters required to completely specify the magnitude and direction of the Earth's magnetic field at any point on its surface.
Hinglish Detail: Magnetic elements wo rashiyaan (parameters) hain jo kisi bhi jagah par Prithvi ke magnetic field ki puri jaankari (taqat aur disha) dene ke liye zaroori hoti hain.
  • Declination ($\theta$)
  • Inclination or Dip ($\delta$)
  • Horizontal ($B_H$) and Vertical ($B_V$) Components.
$$ \text{Dec, Dip, } B_H, B_V $$

6. Magnetic Declination

Detailed English: It is the angle between the Geographic Meridian (true North-South) and the Magnetic Meridian (where the compass points) at a particular location.
Hinglish Detail: Geographic meridian aur magnetic meridian ke beech banne wale kon (angle) ko declination kehte hain. Yeh batata hai ki compass 'True North' se kitna bhatak raha hai.
$$ \text{Angle } (\theta) $$

7. Magnetic Inclination (Dip)

Detailed English: Magnetic Inclination or Angle of Dip is the angle that the total magnetic field of the Earth makes with the horizontal line at any place.
Hinglish Detail: Angle of Dip wo angle hai jo Prithvi ka total magnetic field zameen (horizontal) ke saath banata hai. Equator par yeh 0° aur Poles par 90° hota hai.
$$ \tan \delta = \frac{B_V}{B_H} $$

8. Horizontal Component

Detailed English: It is the component of the total magnetic field of the Earth acting in the horizontal direction. It is the part that drives the magnetic compass needle.
Hinglish Detail: Prithvi ke total magnetic field ka wo hissa jo horizontal (zameen ke parallel) disha mein kaam karta hai. Compass ki needle isi component ki wajah se ghoomti hai.
$$ B_H = B \cos \delta $$

9. Vertical Component

Detailed English: It is the component of the total magnetic field of the Earth acting in the vertical (downward or upward) direction.
Hinglish Detail: Prithvi ke total magnetic field ka wo hissa jo vertical (sidha upar ya neeche) disha mein kaam karta hai. Poles par total field isi component ke barabar hota hai.
$$ B_V = B \sin \delta $$

1. Torque on Bar Magnet

Detailed English: When a bar magnet is placed in a uniform magnetic field ($B$), it experiences a turning effect called torque. This torque tries to rotate the magnet to align its magnetic moment ($M$) with the field.
Hinglish Detail: Jab kisi bar magnet ko ek jaise magnetic field ($B$) mein rakha jata hai, toh us par ek ghumane wali taqat lagti hai jise torque kehte hain. Yeh torque magnet ko field ki disha mein seedha karne ki koshish karta hai.
  • Vector form: $\vec{\tau} = \vec{M} \times \vec{B}$.
  • Magnitude: $\tau = MB \sin \theta$.
  • Maximum torque occurs at $\theta = 90^\circ$.
$$ \tau = MB \sin \theta $$

2. Stable Equilibrium

Detailed English: Stable equilibrium occurs when the magnet's magnetic moment is perfectly aligned with the magnetic field ($\theta = 0^\circ$). The potential energy is at its minimum.
Hinglish Detail: Stable equilibrium tab hota hai jab magnet aur field dono ek hi disha mein hote hain ($\theta = 0^\circ$). Is halat mein magnet sabse zyada shant rehta hai kyunki energy sabse kam hoti hai.
  • Condition: $\theta = 0^\circ$.
  • Potential Energy: $U = -MB$ (Minimum).
  • Torque is zero in this state.
$$ U_{min} = -MB $$

3. Unstable Equilibrium

Detailed English: Unstable equilibrium occurs when the magnetic moment is exactly opposite to the magnetic field ($\theta = 180^\circ$). The potential energy is at its maximum value.
Hinglish Detail: Unstable equilibrium tab hota hai jab magnet field ke bilkul ulta rakha ho ($\theta = 180^\circ$). Is waqt torque zero toh hota hai par energy itni zyada hoti hai ki magnet turant palatne ko taiyar rehta hai.
  • Condition: $\theta = 180^\circ$.
  • Potential Energy: $U = +MB$ (Maximum).
  • Slight disturbance will flip the magnet.
$$ U_{max} = +MB $$

4. Magnetic Materials

Detailed English: Magnetic materials are substances that show a response when kept in an external magnetic field. Their behavior depends on the alignment of atomic dipoles.
Hinglish Detail: Magnetic materials wo cheezein hain jinhe magnetic field mein rakhne par wo reaction dikhati hain. Ye depend karta hai ki unke andar ke chhote dipoles kaise align ho rahe hain.
  • Based on Magnetic Susceptibility ($\chi$).
  • Determines how easily a material can be magnetized.
$$ \vec{M} = \chi \vec{H} $$

5. Classification

Detailed English: Materials are classified based on their reaction to magnetic fields and how their magnetization changes with temperature and field strength.
Hinglish Detail: Materials ko unke magnetic field ke prati bartaav (behavior) ke aadhar par teen mukhya bhagon mein banta gaya hai: Dia, Para aur Ferro.
  • Diamagnetic (Weak Repulsion).
  • Paramagnetic (Weak Attraction).
  • Ferromagnetic (Strong Attraction).
$$ Dia, Para, Ferro $$

6. Diamagnetic Substances

Detailed English: These materials develop weak magnetization opposite to the external field. They are feebly repelled by magnets.
Hinglish Detail: Ye materials magnetic field ke ulte disha mein magnetize hote hain. Magnet inhein halka sa door dhakelta hai.
  • $\chi$ is negative and small.
  • Relative permeability ($\mu_r$) < 1.
  • Example: Water, Copper, Bismuth.
$$ \chi < 0 $$

7. Paramagnetic Substances

Detailed English: These materials develop weak magnetization in the same direction as the field. They are feebly attracted by magnets.
Hinglish Detail: Ye materials field ki disha mein hi halke se magnetize hote hain. Magnet inhein apni taraf thoda sa khinchta hai.
  • $\chi$ is positive and small.
  • $\mu_r$ is slightly greater than 1.
  • Example: Aluminium, Sodium, Platinum.
$$ \chi > 0 \text{ (Small)} $$

8. Ferromagnetic Substances

Detailed English: These materials get strongly magnetized in the direction of the field and are strongly attracted by magnets.
Hinglish Detail: Ye materials field ki disha mein bahut zor se magnetize hote hain aur magnet inhein bahut tezi se apni taraf khinchta hai.
  • $\chi$ is very large and positive.
  • $\mu_r \gg 1$.
  • Example: Iron, Nickel, Cobalt.
$$ \mu_r \gg 1 $$

1. Magnetization

Detailed English: Magnetization ($I$) is defined as the net magnetic dipole moment per unit volume of a material. It represents the extent to which a specimen is magnetized.
Hinglish Detail: Kisi material ke unit volume mein kitna total magnetic dipole moment paida hua hai, usey Magnetization ($I$) kehte hain. Ye batata hai ki material kitna magnetize ho chuka hai.
  • Formula: $I = M_{net} / V$.
  • SI Unit: Ampere/meter ($A/m$).
  • It is a vector quantity.
$$ I = \frac{M}{V} $$

2. Magnetic Intensity

Detailed English: Magnetic Intensity ($H$) is the capability of an external magnetic field to magnetize a material. It depends on the external currents and not on the material itself.
Hinglish Detail: Magnetic Intensity ($H$) wo taqat hai jo kisi material ko magnetize karne ki koshish karti hai. Ye bahar se lagaye gaye field ki strength hoti hai.
  • SI Unit: Ampere/meter ($A/m$).
  • Relation with B: $B = \mu H$.
  • Independent of the medium's properties.
$$ H = \frac{B_0}{\mu_0} $$

3. Magnetic Susceptibility

Detailed English: Magnetic Susceptibility ($\chi$) measures how easily a substance can be magnetized in an external field. It is the ratio of Magnetization ($I$) to Intensity ($H$).
Hinglish Detail: Susceptibility ($\chi$) ye batati hai ki koi material kitni aasani se magnet ban sakta hai. Agar ye zyada hai, toh material jaldi magnetize hoga.
  • Formula: $\chi = I / H$.
  • It is a dimensionless quantity (no unit).
  • Negative for Diamagnetic, Positive for Para/Ferro.
$$ \chi = \frac{I}{H} $$

4. Magnetic Permeability

Detailed English: Magnetic permeability is the ability of a material to allow magnetic lines of force to pass through it.
Hinglish Detail: Permeability ($ \mu $) ka matlab hai ki koi material apne andar se magnetic lines ko kitni aasani se guzarne deta hai.
  • Relative Permeability: $\mu_r = \mu / \mu_0$.
  • Relation with Susceptibility: $\mu_r = 1 + \chi$.
  • For Ferromagnetic materials, $\mu$ is very high.
$$ \mu = \mu_0(1 + \chi) $$

5. Hysteresis Curve

Detailed English: The Hysteresis Curve (B-H loop) shows that the magnetic flux density ($B$) lags behind the magnetic intensity ($H$) during magnetization and demagnetization.
Hinglish Detail: Hysteresis Curve ye dikhata hai ki jab hum magnetic field badhate ya ghatate hain, toh material ka magnetism ($B$) piche reh jata hai. Is cycle mein energy heat ke roop mein loss hoti hai.
  • Area of the loop = Energy loss per cycle.
  • Used to select materials for transformers and magnets.
$$ B \text{ lags behind } H $$

6. Retentivity

Detailed English: Retentivity (Residual Magnetism) is the amount of magnetization remaining in the material when the external magnetic intensity ($H$) is reduced to zero.
Hinglish Detail: Jab bahar ka magnetic field ($H$) zero kar diya jaye, tab bhi material ke andar jo magnetism bacha reh jata hai, usey Retentivity kehte hain.
  • High for Steel (used for permanent magnets).
  • Represents the 'memory' of the material.
$$ B \neq 0 \text{ when } H = 0 $$

7. Coercivity

Detailed English: Coercivity is the reverse magnetic intensity ($H$) required to completely demagnetize the material (make the residual magnetism zero).
Hinglish Detail: Bache huye magnetism ko khatam karne ke liye jitna 'ulta' magnetic field lagana padta hai, usey Coercivity kehte hain.
  • High Coercivity = Strong Permanent Magnet.
  • Low Coercivity = Soft Iron (Easy to demagnetize).
$$ H_c \text{ for } B = 0 $$

1. Permanent Magnets

Detailed English: Permanent magnets are materials that retain their ferromagnetic properties for a long time at room temperature. They require high retentivity so the magnet is strong, and high coercivity so it doesn't lose magnetism easily.
Hinglish Detail: Permanent magnets wo hote hain jo apna magnetism lambe samay tak barkarar rakhte hain. Inhein banane ke liye aisa material chahiye jiski retentivity high ho (taaki magnet mazboot ho) aur coercivity bhi high ho (taaki wo jaldi demagnetize na ho).
  • High Retentivity (Strong residual magnetism).
  • High Coercivity (Resistant to magnetic loss).
  • Example: Steel, Alnico (Aluminium, Nickel, Cobalt).
Steel / Alnico

2. Electromagnets

Detailed English: An electromagnet consists of a core of magnetic material placed inside a solenoid. It behaves as a magnet only as long as electric current flows through the coil. It requires high permeability and low coercivity.
Hinglish Detail: Electromagnet tabhi tak magnet rehta hai jab tak usme current flow hota hai. Iske liye Soft Iron ka use hota hai kyunki iski permeability bahut zyada hoti hai (jaldi magnetize hota hai) aur coercivity kam hoti hai (current band hote hi magnetism khatam).
  • High Permeability (Easy to magnetize).
  • Low Coercivity (Loses magnetism quickly).
  • Example: Soft Iron core in a solenoid.
Soft Iron Core

3. Comparison

Detailed English: Permanent magnets have fixed strength and polarity, whereas electromagnets' strength can be controlled by current and their poles can be reversed. Permanent magnets use Steel; Electromagnets use Soft Iron.
Hinglish Detail: Permanent magnet ki taqat fix rehti hai, par electromagnet ki taqat current badha kar badhayi ja sakti hai. Permanent magnet ko demagnetize karna mushkil hai, jabki electromagnet switch off karte hi khatam ho jata hai.
  • Steel: High Retentivity, High Coercivity.
  • Soft Iron: High Retentivity, Low Coercivity.
  • Electromagnets are used in electric bells and cranes.
Steel vs Soft Iron

Chapter 5: Magnetism & Matter (Top 10)

Q1. Define Magnetic Declination, Angle of Dip, and Horizontal Component of Earth's Magnetic Field.

1. Magnetic Declination (θ): The angle between the geographic meridian and the magnetic meridian at a place.
2. Magnetic Dip (δ): The angle that the total magnetic field of the earth makes with the horizontal line.
3. Horizontal Component (BH): The component of the earth's magnetic field in the horizontal direction.
Formula: BH = B cos δ ; BV = B sin δ ; tan δ = BV / BH

Q2. Prove that a current-carrying solenoid behaves like a bar magnet (Axial Field Derivation).

1. Consider solenoid length 2L, radius 'a', n turns per unit length.
2. Field due to small element dx: dB = [μ₀ n dx I a²] / 2(r-x)³.
3. For r >> x and r >> a, integrate from -L to +L.
4. Result: B = (μ₀ / 4π) × (2M / r³).
5. This is the same as a bar magnet's axial field, proving the equivalence.

Q4. State Gauss's Law in Magnetism. What is its physical significance?

1. Statement: The net magnetic flux through any closed surface is always zero. ∮ B · dA = 0.
2. Significance: It proves that isolated magnetic poles (monopoles) do not exist. Magnetic poles always exist in pairs (North and South).

Q5. Derive the Torque acting on a magnetic dipole in a uniform magnetic field.

1. Consider a magnet of length 2L and pole strength 'm' in field B.
2. Force on North pole = mB, Force on South pole = -mB.
3. Torque τ = Force × Perpendicular distance.
4. τ = mB × (2L sin θ) = (m × 2L) B sin θ.
5. Since M = m × 2L, τ = MB sin θ or τ = M × B.

Q6. Derive Potential Energy of a magnetic dipole in a uniform magnetic field.

1. Work done in rotating dipole by angle dθ: dW = τ dθ.
2. dW = MB sin θ dθ.
3. Integrate from θ₁ to θ₂: W = ∫ MB sin θ dθ = -MB [cos θ₂ - cos θ₁].
4. If θ₁ = 90°, then U = -MB cos θ or U = -M · B.

Q7. State Curie’s Law for paramagnetic substances. What is Curie Temperature?

1. Curie's Law: Susceptibility (χ) is inversely proportional to absolute temperature (T).
2. χ = C / T (where C is Curie constant).
3. Curie Temperature (TC): The transition temperature above which a ferromagnetic material becomes paramagnetic.

Q8. Explain the terms: Retentivity and Coercivity using a Hysteresis Loop.

1. Retentivity: The value of magnetic field (B) left in the material when the external magnetizing field (H) is zero.
2. Coercivity: The reverse magnetizing field (H) required to make the residual magnetism (B) zero.
3. Steel has high coercivity (used for permanent magnets); Soft iron has low coercivity.

Q9. What are the characteristics of materials used for making Electromagnets?

1. High Permeability: To easily concentrate magnetic field lines.
2. Low Retentivity: So magnetism is lost immediately when current is switched off.
3. Low Coercivity: To reduce energy loss.
4. Example: Soft Iron is the best material.

Q10. Derive the relation between Magnetic Susceptibility (χ) and Relative Permeability (μᵣ).

1. Total magnetic field B = B₀ + Bm.
2. B = μ₀H + μ₀I = μ₀(H + I).
3. Since I = χH, we get B = μ₀(H + χH) = μ₀H(1 + χ).
4. Also B = μH = μ₀μᵣH.
5. Comparing both: μᵣ = 1 + χ.
